Homework

  • Turn in a written report about your research project. Your report should follow the model of a research report for Science magazine:

Notes on Homework:

Your report should include the following: * Title: Maximum of 90 characters * Abstract: A one-paragraph abstract that explains to a general reader why you did this project, what the big results were, and what important things they show us regarding your research question. * Text: Up to 2000 words (around eight pages, double-spaced), not including the abstract, references, notes, or captions. Up to four figures or tables.

The text should: * Give a short introduction to the research question and explain why it’s interesting, * Describe the methods you used to answer the question (the model design, the experiments you conducted, and the methods you used to analyze the output from the experiments), * Describe the results of your experiments (figures or tables are useful here), * Discuss the significance of the results with respect to your research question * Present short conclusions about what you learned about your research question and what the next steps for future research using your model might be. * References: In your text, you should cite sources that you used (e.g., the source of the model you modified, books or articles you consulted to learn more about the subject of your study, and sources of authority to support assertions that you make in the text). You may use any standard reference format (e.g., Chicago, APA, MLA, etc.) and you may use either numbered or author-date style citations. * Supporting Materials: You should have a separate folder within your project folder called “Supporting Materials” where you should put the final .nlogo file for your model, output files from your experimental runs, any input data files you used in running your model (e.g., something like the digital elevation map we used in the butterfly mating model), and any additional material that would allow someone else to reproduce your research. The ODD for your model should be in the “Info” pane of your model, but you may also submit a text document with the ODD if you wish as part of the Supporting Materials.

You may also optionally include a supplementary document with text, figures, and tables that don’t fit into the formal report. If you produce a lot of figures or tables, this is a good place to put them.

You may refer to the supplementary materials in the text of your report, but the main text of your paper should stand alone in describing the problem, the general approach you took with your model and experiments, what you found, and why it was significant. The supporting material is for providing extra details that don’t fit into the main paper and for providing the code and data necessary for someone else to reproduce and extend your research.

The main paper should be in a Word or PDF document with the filename <lastname>_final_paper.docx or <lastname>_final_paper.pdf (with your own last name in place of <lastname>). Make a folder in Box called Final_Paper and put your paper there. Make a subfolder inside Final_Paper called Supporting_Material and put all of your supporting material there (ODD document, NetLogo model, etc.).
